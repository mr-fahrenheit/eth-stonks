2. basics

2.1) key terms

the stonk market is a special variable which:

  • starts out at 864 billion
  • never decreases
  • increases by 10% of the stonk value for a buy
  • increases by 100% of the stonks sold
this behavior regulates the market using hyperinflation.

the stonk fund is the usdt available for when stonks are sold.

the bailout fund is the usdt set aside for bailouts.

circuit breakers occur when the stonk market reaches certain values.

bailouts are bonus payouts for each circuit breaker.

stages are the sections of time before each circuit breaker.

2.2) actions

users can make three actions:
  • buy companies for usdt
  • reinvest stonks into new companies
  • sell stonks for usdt

companies cannot be sold and produce 1 stonk per second.
each company produces 1 stonk per second, or 86400 per day.
when reinvesting, 86400 stonks = 1 company.

2.3) market behavior

buying increases stonk value
- stonk fund increases
- minor inflation

reinvesting preserves stonk value
- stonk fund unchanged
- no inflation

selling decreases stonk value
- stonk fund decreases
- hyperinflation

3. fundamentals

3.1) pre-market

the pre-market is a 72 hour period at the start of each round.
all pre-market buys are combined to form the first buy of the round, and the companies received are distributed proportionally.
stonks are not produced until the pre-market is over.

pre-market buyers receive pre-market divs for the entire round.

3.3) circuit breakers and bailouts

as the stonk market grows, it can hit up to three circuit breakers:

  1. 10 quadrillion (1e16)
  2. 10 septillion (1e25)
  3. 10 undecillion (1e37)


whenever a circuit breaker is hit, 7 bailouts are paid:

  • 70% for the biggest spender (entire round so far)
  • 20% for the last 5 buys (4% each)
  • 10% for the biggest producer (most companies)

for the first two circuit breakers, 1/3 of the bailout fund is used.
for the third, the entire bailout fund is used and the round ends.

if there is a market crash, the round ends early and the bailout fund is moved to the stonk fund as "neutral balance" for the next round.

3.4) market crash

the market crash timer prevents the round from ending early.
it has a maximum of 72 hours and starts counting down when the pre-market closes.

time is added when players buy:

  • stage 1: 30 minutes per $1 ($48 / 24h)
  • stage 2: 10 minutes per $1 ($144 / 24h)
  • stage 3: 1 minute per $1 ($1440 / 24h)

4. stonkbrokers

4.1) stonkbroker status

stonkbroker status is earned by spending $1000+ within one round.
it is permanent and persists for later rounds.

if you spend $1000+ in your first buy, you do not need to use a stonkbroker.
stonkbroker status and 10% cashback will be received automatically.

free stonkbroker status can be granted by Mr F at his discretion, and is automatically provided for all beta testers.

4.2) stonkbroker benefits

stonkbrokers receive 10% cashback when buying and can broker trades for non-brokers.

10% of the funds from a brokered trade are split between the buyer and the broker, earning the buyer 5% cashback.

non brokered trades receive no cashback.
therefore, non-brokers must choose between:

  • using a broker
  • becoming a broker
  • losing money
  • leaving the website in disgust

however, non-broker buyers can change their broker.
this means brokers who do not regularly contribute risk losing business.

4.3) using a stonkbroker

to use a stonkbroker, visit their referral link in your browser.
their name will appear underneath "current broker" on the page.

4.4) the chadbroker


the chadbroker receives 1% of all buys and sells after the pre-market closes.

to become the new chadbroker, a stonkbroker must have:

  • more brokered trades than the current chadbroker
  • more earned from brokered trades than the current chadbroker

the chadbroker is reset with each new round.
